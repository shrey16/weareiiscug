Undergraduate Talent Show
Preetham Venkatesh | July, 2017

Ah, UGTS. The word has so many memories associated with it, it’d be difficult to encompass it all in one article. The Undergraduate Talent Show is an annual tradition of IISc UG where the students of the first year, occasionally aided by their seniors, put together a show for the rest of the IISc community. With traditional talents like dance, music and drama, to unconventional events like juggling, comedy skits and standup, the event is intended for people to step out of their comfort zone and bring out their creative side.

However, the impact of UGTS goes beyond just the 2-3 hours spent on stage on the day of the event. It is an opportunity for the entire batch to come together to create something of their own, to learn about event organization as well as try out new things. The planning for UGTS begins nearly a month before the event. First, couple of students take up the responsibility of being the main coordinators of the event. Then, coordinators for different sub-events, like dance, drama, music etc. are chosen. This is accompanied by various tasks like speaking to the UG office about funding, getting SDA on the decided date, having a budget and collecting money, and arranging for a music system. The event also needs a name (like Asterisk, or Winterference)!

The crux of the event lies in, of course, fun and participation. People are often reluctant to participate due to a variety of reasons, from stage fright to studies to the most common, “I have no talent.” Yet, as we’ve seen over the years, it just takes the right platform and a little bit of push to convince people of what they can do. So whether it is having a dance for first timers, or creating a parody “UG Talentless Show” (this is what UG 2016 did) there is always a way to get people to participate! It is also impressive to see the talents people in your batch have. There were some great dancers, musicians and comedians whose abilities were introduced to the rest of the IISc community through UGTS.

Sounds like a lot of work, but it really is a great learning process, and it is fascinating to see it all come together. It leaves the participants, coordinators and the viewers with a memory to cherish. Being one of the few non-academic events for the UG community, it is important to take the tradition forward and ensure a great UGTS experience for all involved. Don’t hesitate to experiment, to try something for the first time, because UGTS is about making sure your life in IISc goes beyond academics; it’s about having nurturing and discovering yourself.
