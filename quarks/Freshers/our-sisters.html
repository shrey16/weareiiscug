Our sisters
Dhanya Bharath | July, 2017

1. NCBS - National Center for Biological Sciences

The National Centre for Biological Sciences is a daughter institute of the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Mumbai. It has a beautiful campus and enthusiastic people from diverse backgrounds – physicists and mathematicians included – working on intriguing biological problems, making this institute truly interdisciplinary. The canteen here is a welcome haven for people who love good, cheap food. Besides, several lectures and seminars are held frequently providing an excellent opportunity to interact with eminent scientists.

2. ICTS - International Center for Theoretical Sciences

The International Centre for Theoretical Sciences is another daughter institute of TIFR, Mumbai. With an excellent telescope facilities, it is a must-visit place for all Astronomy enthusiasts. In collaboration with the Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium, it arranges popular science talks under the title ‘Kaapi with Kuriosity”. (Kaapi refers to coffee in local slang). Though supposedly catering to the general public, these talks are often a source of inspiration and motivation for budding scientists like us.

3. JNCASR - Jawaharlal Nehru Center for Advanced Scientific Research

The Jawaharlal Nehru Centre for Advanced Scientific Research is a multidisciplinary research institute situated in Jakkur, an area in North Bangalore. People here work at the frontiers of Science and Engineering on a wide variety of areas. Though relatively young, this institute is well-known all over the world.

4. RRI - Raman Research Institute

The Raman Research Institute, founded by Prof. C V Raman is a leading research institute in the fields of Astronomy and Astrophysics, Light and Matter Physics, Soft Condensed Matter and Theoretical Physics. There are regular shuttles from our campus to the above sister institutes. Timings can be verified at the Security Office near the Prakruthi gate. Professors at any of these institutes can be approached for summer projects.
