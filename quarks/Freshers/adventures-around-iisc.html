Adventures Around IISc
Durjay Pramanik | July, 2017

Hello juniors, first of all, my heartiest congratulations on your admission into one of the most prestigious institutes in India. In the following four or five years you will get to know the real challenges of studying in IISc. Mark my words, it’s not going to be easy, but it will make you capable. Going through this hard time, you will need good friends and some hobbies. For me, its travelling and adventure. When I get bored of the tedious and repetitious coursework, or feeling down; I turn myself in to this hobby of mine, and it gives me peace of mind. It’s not like we plan everything out; believe me, in college life, the most planned trips are most likely not to occur.

I also expect some of you to be nature-lovers, travellers, and adventurers. So, here are some places you can visit in around 12 hours time in and around Bangalore to quench your wanderlust a little.

I went to these places by bike on weekend or holiday nights. But there are other means of travel like renting a self-drive car (eg. Zoomcar) or bike (eg. Wheelstreet) or cycling or public transportation etc.

Nandi hills (Distance: 58km)

This is the nearest and most popular out-of-the-city tourist spot you can visit, I guess all IIScians have been there at least once. Going to this place is easy and comfortable, it’s good to start with. If you reach there early in the morning, you will be able to see clouds passing by, the cold wind and gentle rays of rising sun comforting you. From the top everything looks like it belongs to a miniature toy park. Maybe that’s why Tipu sultan built a summer palace there, which you can still see, at least whatever is left of it. When you come down from the hill, you must try the roadside food.

Avalabetta (Distance: 90km)

After visiting Nandi hills if you want to go a little further and to a place less popular, Avalabetta should be on your list. As the place is not visited often, roads are not well maintained. You have to go on a 10km dusty road in between forest, far from the nearest village. Riding through this place for the first time, at midnight chilled me to the core. Do not trust Google Maps blindly at this point. Do stop on this road, and you will be able to hear the pleasing sounds of insects and alarming howling, roaring of animals. Then as you look up and find a clear sky full of stars, you’ll want to leave everything and keep gazing forever. All these things give you an adrenaline rush and a sense of freedom for the first time which is too hard to turn down. Then you just feel the irresistible urge to go on this kind of adventures.

When you reach at the gate at around 4 am, there may be some vendors selling tea, coffee and different varieties of hot fries which is just perfect. Then you wait for some time staring at the sky or chatting with you friends or with the few people who went there from Bangalore to seek similar adventures like you. Now you have to make a brave decision, if you want to see the sunrise. The gate will be closed and there will be no one inside, you will have to make your way pass that closed gate and walk to the top in the dark. The view of the sunrise is going to be phenomenal. If you can gather some courage, luck and finish this trip, it will be like no other.

Madhugiri Fort (Distance: 97km)

This is going to be a hard trek, so prepare yourself for almost two hours of steep, tiring trek. If you leave IISc at 3 am, you will reach there around 5 am. Have some biscuit and the awesome coffee at the stall near the bus stand, then start your trek. At first there will be stairs and railing but as you go up it will be harder and harder, and you will find yourself trekking very steep rocks without any support. However, exhausting the trek is, when you reach the top finally all of your efforts will be rewarded. The surrounding is so peaceful and relaxing you will just forget about everything. Just sit the on the walls on the side walls, feel the pleasing wind and enjoy the view of the town nearby and the other hills. I am sure you will lose track of time.

Savandurga (Distance: 63km)

This is a place where you will find no restriction to climb the hill unlike the other three. You start your trip at 1am you will reach there by 3am. After taking some rest you climb up on the hill and sit at the top to enjoy the awesome view of the towns nearby and the other hills. The sky will be full of stars and if you go there on a full moon day, the experience gets even better. The sun rises from in between two hills far away. When you descend, some stalls will be open, where you can have some coffee and start your return journey.

Rasta Café (Distance: 47km)

Sometimes you just feel like going on a bike ride at night without any tiring trek or spending a lot of time. The way to Rasta café is one of the nicest night bike ride I had. While riding on the four lane Outer Ring road with very little traffic, you will be able to see the horizon kissed by the city lights, while the lights of the industrial buildings decorate the rest of your visor. The food, and the ambience is decent there. The place will be open till 4am, and you can come back by 5am.

There are a lot of other places you can explore depending on availability of your time. I just wanted to give you all a feel of travelling; What I just wrote is only the tip of the iceberg.
